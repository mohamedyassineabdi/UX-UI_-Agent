Cohérence UI & Design System | Accueil

Verbs are used for all actions (e.g. Save, Go, Submit, Continue).

Issue: On Accueil, the audit found that 'Verbs are used for all actions (e.g. Save, Go, Submit, Continue).' is not fully met. Action labels do not pass the verb criterion. At least one page relies too heavily on non-verb action labels.

Why it matters: This matters because recurring patterns do not behave or look consistently, which makes the product feel less mature. In a GTM context, visible friction on Accueil can make the product feel harder to understand, trust, or adopt during a first review.

Evidence: Action labels do not pass the verb criterion At least one page relies too heavily on non-verb action labels.

What to change: Prefer direct verb-led action labels such as Search, Add to cart, Continue, Save, or Send.

Evidence: User control over flows does not pass The available signals do not strongly support user control over interactive flows.

Cohérence UI & Design System | Accueil

The UI (and all buttons or controls) responds consistently to user actions in terms of visual display, appropriate context and data functionality.

Issue: On Accueil, the audit found that 'The UI (and all buttons or controls) responds consistently to user actions in terms of visual display, appropriate context and data functionality.' is not fully met. Control response consistency does not pass. The visible control styles are too inconsistent to support reliable interaction behavior expectations.

Why it matters: This matters because recurring patterns do not behave or look consistently, which makes the product feel less mature. In a GTM context, visible friction on Accueil can make the product feel harder to understand, trust, or adopt during a first review.

Evidence: Control response consistency does not pass The visible control styles are too inconsistent to support reliable interaction behavior expectations.

What to change: Standardize control variants and add runtime hover, focus, active, and disabled-state extraction.

Issue: On Home, the audit found that 'Search is available on every page, not just the homepage.' is not fully met. Actual site-search inputs detected on every page was not found.

Why it matters: This matters because prospects may not immediately understand why this product is relevant for their context or market. In a GTM context, visible friction on Home can make the product feel harder to understand, trust, or adopt during a first review.

Evidence: Actual site-search inputs detected on every page=False.

What to change: Fix this first on Home by clarifying the interaction, tightening the label or feedback, and making the intended next step more obvious.

Logique de Flux & Architecture | Accueil

Frequently used features are readily available.

Issue: On Accueil, the audit found that 'Frequently used features are readily available.' is not fully met. Frequently used features are not surfaced strongly enough. Frequently used controls are not sufficiently visible in top or navigation zones.

Why it matters: This matters because people struggle to understand where they are, what comes next, or how the product is organized. In a GTM context, visible friction on Accueil can make the product feel harder to understand, trust, or adopt during a first review.

Evidence: Frequently used features are not surfaced strongly enough Frequently used controls are not sufficiently visible in top or navigation zones.

What to change: Expose high-frequency features such as search, filter, cart, account, and menu in predictable top or navigation regions.
